## Our Declaration

We exist to build tools that empower working people. When applied to benefit the  collective, technology becomes a catalyst that enables communities to flourish.  

We're here to show what a worker-owned future can look like.  

### Who We Are

We are from all over the world, from every expertise, united by the idea of the world we can  put in place. Using technology to provide a better life for everyone, everywhere.  

We are **worker-owned**. Everyone who contributes has a say in how things work. Everyone  shares in what we build together.  

We are **funded by the tools we build and the projects we take on**. No venture capital, no  investors, and not beholden to shareholder value.  

### What We Build

**Practical financial tools** that put working people in control:  

- Send money to family and friends without banks taking huge cuts    
- Save together with people who have your back when times get tough  \- Pool resources to weather storms and build collective wealth  

- Access services designed for real life, not just people with perfect credit  \- Make decisions about money with transparency and community input  

Every tool gets better when more people use it. We're building **abundance, not scarcity**.  

### How We Work

**Built together, owned together.** We make decisions collectively because the people using  these tools should control how they work.  

**Transparent by design.** We publish how our tools work, how money flows, and how  decisions get made. Questions make everything better.  

**Tested with real people.** These aren't theories or prototypes. Real communities use these  tools every day to manage real money in real situations.  

**Resilient enough for real life.** Our tools work when other systems fail. They're designed for  the world as it is, while building the world as it could be.  

**Mass appeal without compromise.** We build for everyone, but we never forget who we  serve first: working people who need financial tools that actually work.   

### Why This Matters Now

The world today is the result of choices made. Many of these outside our control. We want  to empower working people to make their own choices, especially in communities where  few have previously existed.  We use technology to put these choices in place. Tools need to serve the needs of real  people, and our mission is to make them accessible and effective.  

This is practical optimism, and we don't wait for permission to fix problems. Everything we  do is informed by systems that have historically benefitted people. We are building on these  ideas to empower workers in the digital age.

When working people control their own financial tools, we can:  

- Keep more of what we earn    
- Support each other through difficult times    
- Build wealth collectively instead of competitively    
- Make financial decisions based on community benefit, not individual profit  \- Create systems that serve human needs first

## Our Commitment

**To working people everywhere:** We build tools for you, controlled by people like you,  designed for the challenges you actually face.  

**To our cooperatives:** We maintain democratic control, transparent operations, and  equitable distribution of benefits.  

**To our communities:** We share knowledge freely, support other cooperative efforts, and  measure our success by collective benefit.  

**To the future:** We're building financial infrastructure that can serve as the foundation for a  more equitable economy.  

### The Vision We're Building Toward

Imagine tools that:  

- Are designed for cooperation, not competition    
- Get better when more people use them    
- Generate wealth for communities, not just individuals    
- Work the way you actually live and work    
- Are controlled by the people who use them  

**This isn't utopian thinking. This is practical building.** 

We know these systems can work because we're building them. Every tool we deploy  proves that we can organize things differently.   

### Core Principles That Guide Everything We Do  

#### 1\. Human-First Technology  

Technology serves human needs. We build tools that make people's lives better and easier.  When people needed ways to pool resources for mutual support, we built crowdstaking  systems. 

#### 2\. Cooperative Control  

The people who use tools control how they work. Democratic decision-making is how we  operate. Our core members share multisig control of funds, with spending limits and  collective financial decisions that give everyone real ownership with governance power, not  just equity on paper.  

#### 3\. Practical Solidarity  

Solidarity is the core of Bread Cooperative. When working people cooperate, we can build  abundance and resilience that benefits everyone. We've supported people evacuating  active warzones, built aid systems for communities facing famine, and created mutual aid  networks among self-employed people \- because solidarity means showing up when it  matters most.  

#### 4\. Right to Privacy  

People have the right to private communication and private technology. Privacy is  fundamental to human agency and accountability. We support privacy technology for  unions, protesters, and whistleblowers, and defend the right to financial privacy as essential  for human agency and holding power accountable.  

#### 5\. Resilient Design  

Our tools work when other systems fail. We adapt to the human needs of the moment.  When traditional funding systems couldn't reach crisis zones, our tools enabled direct peer to-peer support. When existing financial infrastructure excluded people, we built  alternatives that work for real communities.  

#### 6\. Sustainable Growth  

We grow by strengthening communities. To us, success means more funding for  cooperatives doing essential work, and fostering cycles of growth for the collective.

## Our Manifesto in Action

We start with real problems working people face with their money, then build tools that  actually solve them.  

People and communities affected by decisions are the drivers of solutions.  

Every dollar that flows through our system is accounted for transparently, with resources  distributed in line with cooperative principles.  

Every member has the same access to information, the same voice in decisions and the  same share of benefits available to all. We are owners, not participants.   
Every challenge is viewed collectively, and all problem-solving responsibilities are shared.  We overcome obstacles together.  

This is what it looks like when working people take control of their own financial future. This  is solidarity made practical.
